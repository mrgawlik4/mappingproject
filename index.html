Bell's Diner

Bell's originated as a classic, American diner that served your breakfast staples: Pancakes, eggs, bacon. When a local Korean American family bought the restaurant, they kept those diner classics while adding a panoply of Korean dishes to the menu.

Bell's is the perfect place to mix and match dishes. Get some bibimbop with a side of toast. Don't miss the spicy pork, either.

Evergreen

Established in 1992, Evergreen Restuarant has perfected the art of Chinese cooking. The restaurant offers Mandarin, Sizchuan, Hunan, Shanghai and Taiwan dishes, along with a full bar stocked with wine, beer, sake and liquor. Dine in for a tranquil meal in Evergreen's airy dining room, or grab takeout to eat at home.

The cashew chicken is one of the menus stand out items, as are the Chinese Oriental Burger, which is available with both lamb or pork. When dining in, make sure to get a pot of Jasmine Tea.

Frita Batidos

Frita Batidos' name cpmes from its two signature items: fritas, or Cuban-inspired burgers, and batidos, frozen cocktails made with light rum. Customers sit in a white-washed brick dining room at communal tables.

Served with sweet chili mayo, the beef frita is unmatched, especially when topped with homemade tropical slaw. Grab a passion fruit batido and a side of fried plantains to finish your meal.

Pacific Rim

The priciest entry on this list, Pacific Rim is a restaurant for special occassions—which makes eating there all the more exciting.

TeaHaus

When you walk into TeaHaus, you're greated by an entire wall of tea canisters. The cafe sells loose leaf tea for home use, but also has a dining area if you want to sit down with a pot.

And TeaHaus sells more than beverages, offering a huge selection of baked goods and pastries. Most famous are the cafe's French macarons, which change flavors every month (some favorites from the past: pineapple, salted chai, and Cap'n Crunch).

Hello Faz

Founder Faz Husein might have passed away in 2006, but he still looms large over his Halal pizzeria; on the restaurant's wall, there are dozens of pictures of Faz meeting Bill Clinton, Mother Theresa and the Dalai Lama (among others).

There are endless options at Hello Faz, but the tandoori chicken pizza is unlike anything you'll get at a standard pizzeria. Don't miss the cinnamon bread, either

Tomukun BBQ

Tomukun is actually a two-for-one restaurant: The right side is a noodle bar, and the left is a Korean BBQ spot. While the noodle bar is wonderful, BBQ is the real standout.

Pick your favorite meats and throw them on the grill, which features at the center of each table. The bulgogi and marinated short ribs are highlights, and all combo platters are served with spreads and sides.

Charley's

On Thursdays, Charley's serves a great burger special that gets you a full meal for about $7. But let's be honest: The real reason people go to Charley's are for the 64 ounce fishbowl cocktails.

Served in oversized mason jars, fishbowls offer distinct fusions of rum, vodka, gin and tequila with everything from fruit juice to iced tea. Favorite combinations include the Love Spell, the Ecto Cooler and the Purple People Eater

Blimpy Burger

Arguably Ann Arbor's most famous restaurant, eating at Blimpy Burger is an experience. Make sure to know what you want before you get to the front of the line, becuase the chefs cook fast (and don't have the most patience).

Start with onion rings or deep fried mushrooms before ordering a Blimpy Burger and loading it with your favorite toppings. Patties are thin, so don't be afraid to get a triple (or a quad, if you're really hungry!).
